On Sunday, May 11, 2008, at approximately 6:49 A.M., uniformed patrol officers in marked vehicles were dispatched on a call regarding a W/M/23 suspect, armed with a handgun, who was off his medication and having suicidal thoughts. The suspect fled in a vehicle and eventually stopped in two lanes of traffic at 800 Stemmons Freeway. The suspect exited his vehicle, placed his hands behind his back and walked towards the officers. The officers gave verbal commands to the suspect and he refused to comply and raised his arm as if pulling something out. Two officers fired their weapons at the suspect striking him 1 time. The suspect was injured and transported to Parkland Hospital. Suspect was unarmed. Suspect was charged with Felony Evading Arrest. No officer was injured. Two officers fired 6 rounds. Involved Officers: W/M/ 26 years, 1 month service, W/M 1 year, 4 months service.
